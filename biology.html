Chapter 1: Cell Biology

The cell is the basic structural and functional unit of all living organisms. Cells are the building blocks of life.

Cells can be prokaryotic or eukaryotic. Eukaryotic cells contain membrane-bound organelles, while prokaryotic cells do not.

Key organelles include:

  • Nucleus: Contains DNA
  • Mitochondria: Produces energy
  • Ribosomes: Synthesize proteins
  • Endoplasmic Reticulum: Modifies proteins

Key Notes

Cells have organelles that perform specific functions, allowing for division of labor within the cell.

DNA is the genetic material that carries hereditary information.
