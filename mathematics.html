Chapter 1: Algebra

Algebra is the branch of mathematics that deals with symbols and the rules for manipulating those symbols. It is a unifying thread of almost all of mathematics.

Algebra involves solving equations, inequalities, and systems of equations. It uses variables to represent unknown quantities and operations to manipulate these variables.

Key concepts in algebra include:

  • Variables and constants
  • Expressions and equations
  • Functions and graphs
  • Polynomials and factoring

Key Notes

Remember the order of operations: PEMDAS (Parentheses, Exponents, Multiplication and Division, Addition and Subtraction).

The quadratic formula is x = [-b ± √(b² - 4ac)] / 2a, used to solve quadratic equations.

Chapter 2: Geometry

Geometry is the branch of mathematics concerned with the properties and relations of points, lines, surfaces, and solids. It deals with shapes, sizes, and the properties of space.

Key concepts include points, lines, planes, angles, triangles, circles, and polygons. Geometry helps us understand the world around us and solve real-world problems.

Key Notes

The Pythagorean theorem states that in a right-angled triangle, the square of the hypotenuse is equal to the sum of the squares of the other two sides: a² + b² = c².

The area of a circle is πr², and the circumference is 2πr. Pi (π) is approximately 3.14159, an irrational number.

Similar triangles have the same shape but different sizes. Their corresponding angles are equal, and their corresponding sides are proportional.

Chapter 3: Trigonometry

Trigonometry is the study of relationships between the angles and sides of triangles. It has applications in physics, engineering, and navigation.

The six trigonometric functions are sine (sin), cosine (cos), tangent (tan), cosecant (csc), secant (sec), and cotangent (cot).

Key Notes

In a right triangle, sin θ = opposite/hypotenuse, cos θ = adjacent/hypotenuse, tan θ = opposite/adjacent.

The unit circle is a circle with radius 1 centered at the origin. It helps visualize trigonometric functions.

Trigonometric identities include Pythagorean identities like sin²θ + cos²θ = 1, and angle sum identities.

Chapter 4: Calculus

Calculus is the mathematical study of continuous change. It has two main branches: differential calculus and integral calculus.

Differential calculus deals with rates of change and slopes of curves. Integral calculus deals with accumulation and areas under curves.

Key Notes

The derivative of a function f(x) is denoted f'(x) or dy/dx, representing the instantaneous rate of change.

The fundamental theorem of calculus links differentiation and integration: the integral of f'(x) is f(x) + C.

Limits are fundamental to calculus, describing the behavior of functions as they approach certain values.

Chapter 5: Statistics

Statistics is the science of collecting, analyzing, interpreting, and presenting data. It helps us make informed decisions based on data.

Key concepts include mean, median, mode, standard deviation, probability, and data visualization.

Key Notes

The mean is the average of a set of numbers. The median is the middle value when numbers are sorted. The mode is the most frequent value.

Probability measures the likelihood of events occurring. It ranges from 0 (impossible) to 1 (certain).

Normal distribution is a bell-shaped curve that describes many natural phenomena. The empirical rule states that 68% of data falls within one standard deviation of the mean.

Chapter 6: Number Theory

Number theory is the study of integers and their properties. It explores prime numbers, divisibility, and modular arithmetic.

Key concepts include prime numbers, greatest common divisor (GCD), least common multiple (LCM), and the fundamental theorem of arithmetic.

Key Notes

A prime number is a natural number greater than 1 that has no positive divisors other than 1 and itself.

The Euclidean algorithm is used to find the GCD of two numbers. LCM can be found using the formula LCM(a,b) = (a*b)/GCD(a,b).

Modular arithmetic deals with remainders. For example, 17 mod 5 = 2, since 17 divided by 5 leaves a remainder of 2.
